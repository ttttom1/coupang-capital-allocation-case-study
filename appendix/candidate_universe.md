# Candidate Universe and Time Horizon

## Why This Appendix Exists

The final scorecard focuses on Product Commerce recovery, Taiwan, Eats/WOW, and Farfetch. Those are not the only initiatives mentioned in Coupang's public materials. They are the initiatives selected from a broader candidate universe because they are most relevant to 2026-2028 capital allocation and have enough public evidence to support a directional decision.

## Why The Scorecard Focuses On Four Main Options

The scorecard uses three filters.

### 1. Materiality

The option needed to matter enough to influence capital allocation. Product Commerce and Developing Offerings are reported segments. Within Developing Offerings, the 2025 Form 10-K says revenues are primarily generated from Farfetch, Eats, and retail operations in Taiwan.

### 2. Decision Relevance

The option needed to create a real management choice: protect, scale, prove, constrain, or benchmark. Product Commerce, Taiwan, Eats/WOW, and Farfetch each imply different capital and management attention decisions.

### 3. Evidence Sufficiency

The option needed enough public evidence to score directionally. Play, fintech, advertising, and Rocket Now in Japan are mentioned, but public sources do not provide enough standalone economics or strategic detail to score them responsibly as full options.

## Why WOW Was Paired With Eats

WOW is not treated as a standalone segment. It is treated as a retention and frequency layer because public materials connect membership, customer engagement, and Product Commerce active customer metrics. The 2025 Form 10-K also discusses regulatory scrutiny around tying Eats benefits to WOW membership.

This makes Eats/WOW a combined flywheel question:

```text
Does Eats plus WOW increase retention, frequency, and LTV after delivery costs, subsidies, and regulatory constraints?
```

## Why Taiwan, Not Taipei

The public sources support analysis at the Taiwan level, not the Taipei city level. The Q1 2026 call discusses Taiwan and Taiwan Rocket Delivery. City-level revenue, cohort, fulfillment-density, or margin data are not public.

## Why 2026-2028

The 2026-2028 horizon is an analyst-defined planning horizon, not a specific Coupang guidance period.

The rationale:

1. 2026 is the current decision year because the latest public quarterly package is Q1 2026.
2. Coupang gave FY2026 Developing Offerings adjusted EBITDA loss guidance of $950M-$1B, making 2026 the loss-budget year.
3. A three-year horizon is long enough to test whether Taiwan, Eats/WOW, and Farfetch show operating leverage, but short enough to avoid speculative long-range forecasting from public data.
4. Q1 2026 commentary points to recovery and margin expansion after near-term incident effects fade, so 2027-2028 are useful years for recovery and scale-gate evaluation.

The horizon should be read as:

```text
2026: stabilize and set gates
2027: test operating leverage and recovery
2028: decide scale, hold, constrain, or restructure based on evidence
```

If internal data were available, the horizon could be changed. A board case might use a 2026-2027 near-term gate plus a 2028-2030 strategic case. A public portfolio case should stay closer to the observable evidence.
