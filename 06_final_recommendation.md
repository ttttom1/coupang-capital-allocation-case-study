# Final Recommendation

## Recommendation

Approve a gated 2026-2028 capital allocation plan: restore Product Commerce margin first, selectively scale Taiwan inside a defined loss budget, keep Eats/WOW tied to measurable LTV proof, and constrain Farfetch until standalone turnaround evidence improves.

## Rationale

Coupang's 2026 constraint is margin capacity. FY2025 showed a strong core and an expensive growth portfolio. Q1 2026 then compressed consolidated profitability: Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA fell by $(192)M YoY, while Developing Offerings losses worsened by $(161)M YoY.

The right answer is not to stop growth. The right answer is to force different growth options through different evidence gates.

## Recommended Portfolio Posture

| Priority | Action | Rationale |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | Protect Product Commerce margin recovery. | It is the funding engine and highest-scoring option. |
| 2 | Selectively scale Taiwan with unit-economics gates. | It has the strongest non-core growth evidence but missing standalone economics. |
| 3 | Keep Eats/WOW tied to LTV proof. | Strategic fit is strong, but contribution economics are not disclosed. |
| 4 | Constrain Farfetch until turnaround evidence improves. | Risk and integration evidence outweigh fresh upside evidence. |
| 5 | Use buybacks and core reinvestment as hurdle benchmarks. | Growth investments should beat risk-adjusted capital-return alternatives. |

## Decision Gates

| Option | Scale if | Hold if | Constrain if |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product Commerce | Q2-Q4 margin and active-customer metrics recover after voucher and capacity effects fade. | Recovery is partial but improving. | Core margin compression persists after temporary effects should have faded. |
| Taiwan | Cohort retention remains strong and loss intensity narrows as fulfillment density improves. | Growth remains strong but standalone economics stay undisclosed. | Losses widen without operating leverage. |
| Eats/WOW | Incremental LTV, retention, or frequency improves after delivery costs and subsidies. | Growth continues but contribution economics are unclear. | Growth depends on subsidies or regulatory pressure weakens bundle economics. |
| Farfetch | Standalone loss contribution shrinks and integration risks improve. | Revenue is stable but profitability proof is incomplete. | Losses remain material and turnaround milestones are not disclosed. |

## Risks And Mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product Commerce recovery is slower than expected | The portfolio has less capacity to absorb growth losses. | Reduce Developing Offerings loss budget and prioritize core margin levers. |
| Taiwan growth is promotion-driven | Revenue growth may not convert into durable economics. | Require cohort retention and loss-intensity improvement before each funding release. |
| Eats/WOW engagement does not translate to LTV | Growth could add cost without increasing customer value. | Fund only experiments that show net LTV after delivery costs and subsidies. |
| Farfetch absorbs management attention | Turnaround complexity could distract from higher-return priorities. | Set hard milestones and trigger restructuring or exit review if missed. |
| Public data masks initiative economics | Current scores may change with internal data. | Replace directional scores with internal dashboards before final capital releases. |
