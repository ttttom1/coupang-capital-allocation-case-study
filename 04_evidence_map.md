# Initiative Evidence Map

## Evidence Strength Scale

| Rating | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| High | Official source gives direct initiative-specific facts or quantified financials. |
| Medium | Official source gives initiative-specific commentary but limited standalone economics. |
| Low | Evidence is indirect, grouped under a segment, or relies on inference. |

## Initiative Summary

| Initiative | Latest public evidence | Evidence strength | Main data gap |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product Commerce | Segment revenue, gross profit, adjusted EBITDA, and management commentary on Q1 incident effects. | High | Recovery trajectory after Q1 temporary effects fade. |
| Taiwan | Management commentary says Developing Offerings growth was primarily driven by Taiwan hypergrowth and early cohorts resemble early Korea. | Medium | Standalone revenue, gross profit, EBITDA, cohort economics. |
| Eats/WOW | Management commentary cites high growth in Eats. 2025 Form 10-K references WOW membership revenue and KFTC scrutiny around Eats benefits. | Low-Medium | Contribution margin, subsidy intensity, delivery cost, incremental LTV, member churn. |
| Farfetch | 2025 Form 10-K identifies Farfetch as material inside Developing Offerings and discloses acquisition/integration risks. | Medium | Standalone profitability trend and turnaround milestones. |

## Product Commerce

Product Commerce has the strongest evidence base because Coupang reports segment revenue, gross profit, and adjusted EBITDA. FY2025 Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA was $2.485B, and Q1 2026 Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA was $358M.

The main uncertainty is recovery. Management commentary attributes Q1 pressure partly to incident-related vouchers, underutilized capacity, and demand dislocation. The recommendation therefore treats Product Commerce as the funding engine but requires Q2-Q4 recovery checks.

## Taiwan

Taiwan is the strongest non-core growth candidate. Management commentary points to Taiwan hypergrowth, strong early customer response, and retention resembling early Korea.

The evidence is not enough for an all-in recommendation. Coupang does not disclose Taiwan standalone revenue, gross profit, EBITDA, loss intensity, or cohort economics. Taiwan should therefore receive staged capital only if retention and loss intensity improve as fulfillment density rises.

## Eats/WOW

Eats/WOW has strong strategic logic. Food delivery and membership benefits can increase customer frequency, retention, and cross-category engagement.

The economics are not proven from public data. Coupang does not disclose Eats contribution margin, subsidy intensity, delivery costs after benefits, WOW member churn, or incremental LTV. The 2025 Form 10-K also identifies regulatory scrutiny around Eats benefits inside WOW membership.

## Farfetch

Farfetch is material inside Developing Offerings, but the public evidence supports risk more clearly than upside. The 2025 Form 10-K discloses acquisition, integration, control, brand, FX, litigation, and regulatory risks.

The Q1 2026 public package does not provide clear standalone evidence that Farfetch losses are shrinking or integration risks are resolved. Farfetch should be managed as a turnaround until evidence improves.
