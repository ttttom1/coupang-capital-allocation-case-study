# Board Deck Outline

## Slide 1: Coupang should adopt a gated capital allocation plan for 2026-2028

**Exhibit:** One-line recommendation and four portfolio decisions.

**Message:** Restore Product Commerce margin first, selectively scale Taiwan, prove Eats/WOW LTV, and constrain Farfetch until turnaround evidence improves.

## Slide 2: Q1 2026 reduced the margin buffer available to fund multiple growth bets

**Exhibit:** EBITDA bridge from Q1 2025 to Q1 2026.

| Driver | Q1 2025 adj. EBITDA | Q1 2026 adj. EBITDA | YoY change |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Product Commerce | $550M | $358M | $(192)M |
| Developing Offerings | $(168)M | $(329)M | $(161)M |
| Consolidated | $382M | $29M | $(353)M |

**Message:** The decline combines Product Commerce pressure and Developing Offerings growth-investment drag.

## Slide 3: Product Commerce is the funding engine and should not be treated as one option among many

**Exhibit:** FY2025 and Q1 2026 Product Commerce performance callout.

**Key facts:**

- FY2025 Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA: $2.485B.
- Q1 2026 Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA: $358M.
- Q1 pressure was tied to vouchers, underutilized capacity, and demand disruption.

**Message:** Management should verify margin recovery before increasing tolerance for Developing Offerings losses.

## Slide 4: Developing Offerings should be managed as separate initiatives, not one growth bucket

**Exhibit:** Initiative evidence map.

| Initiative | Current read | Main gap |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Taiwan | Strongest non-core growth signal | Standalone economics |
| Eats/WOW | Strong flywheel fit | Contribution margin and incremental LTV |
| Farfetch | Material but risk-heavy | Turnaround proof |

**Message:** Different initiatives have different evidence quality, risk, and strategic fit. They need different gates.

## Slide 6: Taiwan should receive staged capital only if unit-economics gates improve

**Exhibit:** Taiwan gate table.

| Gate | Scale if | Hold / slow if |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Growth quality | Retention and repeat behavior remain strong | Growth depends on promotions |
| Unit economics | Loss intensity narrows with density | Losses widen without leverage |
| Operations | Network and last-mile reliability improve | Investment creates persistent complexity |

**Message:** Taiwan is the best growth candidate, but missing standalone economics prevent an all-in recommendation.

## Slide 7: Eats/WOW and Farfetch require different types of discipline

**Exhibit:** Two-column control logic.

| Eats/WOW | Farfetch |
| --- | --- |
| Prove incremental LTV after delivery costs and subsidies | Prove standalone loss reduction |
| Track retention, frequency, and net revenue per active customer | Track integration, control, and restructuring milestones |
| Redesign bundle if regulatory pressure worsens | Escalate to restructuring or exit review if milestones are missed |

**Message:** Eats/WOW is a proof case. Farfetch is a turnaround case.
