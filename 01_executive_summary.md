# Executive Summary

## Recommendation

Coupang should adopt a gated 2026-2028 capital allocation plan: restore Product Commerce margin first, selectively scale Taiwan inside a defined loss budget, keep Eats/WOW tied to measurable LTV proof, and constrain Farfetch until standalone turnaround evidence improves.

This recommendation is not a retreat from growth. It is a sequencing decision. Growth options should earn incremental capital through evidence while the core margin engine recovers.

## Key Facts

| Fact | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| FY2025 Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA was $2.485B. | Product Commerce is the portfolio's funding engine. |
| FY2025 Developing Offerings adjusted EBITDA was $(995)M. | Growth options were already expensive before Q1 2026. |
| Q1 2026 consolidated adjusted EBITDA fell from $382M to $29M YoY. | The margin buffer narrowed sharply. |
| Q1 2026 Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA fell by $(192)M YoY. | Core margin recovery must be verified after incident-related pressure. |
| Q1 2026 Developing Offerings losses worsened by $(161)M YoY. | Growth investment needs budget discipline and initiative-level gates. |

## Option Ranking

## Why This Ranking

Product Commerce ranks first because it has disclosed profit scale and direct strategic fit with Coupang's fulfillment, customer frequency, and membership flywheel.

Taiwan ranks second because it reuses Coupang's core capabilities and has positive management commentary, but standalone revenue, gross profit, and EBITDA are not disclosed.

Eats/WOW has strong strategic logic, but public data do not prove contribution margin, subsidy intensity, delivery-cost economics, or incremental LTV.

Farfetch is financially material but risk-heavy. Public filings show acquisition, integration, control, brand, FX, litigation, and regulatory risks more clearly than turnaround upside.
