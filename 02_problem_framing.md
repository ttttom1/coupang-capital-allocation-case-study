# Problem Framing

## Central Question

Where should Coupang allocate capital and management attention in 2026-2028: Product Commerce margin recovery, Taiwan expansion, Eats/WOW strengthening, or Farfetch normalization?

## Context

Coupang has a strong Product Commerce core and a portfolio of growth options grouped under Developing Offerings. Q1 2026 compressed consolidated profitability, making capital allocation more difficult.

The strategic question is not whether Coupang should grow. The question is which growth options deserve funding while Product Commerce recovers and Developing Offerings absorbs planned losses.

## Scope

Included:

- Product Commerce margin recovery
- Taiwan expansion
- Eats/WOW flywheel
- Farfetch normalization
- Public financial and investor evidence
- Capital allocation posture and decision gates

Excluded:

- Stock valuation
- Investment recommendation
- Proprietary internal forecasts
- Non-public customer or cohort data
- Legal advice on regulatory matters

## Issue Tree

```text
How should Coupang allocate capital and management attention in 2026-2028?

1. Economic Attractiveness
   1.1 Which option protects or creates the most margin capacity?
   1.2 Which option has disclosed or inferable unit economics?
   1.3 Which option consumes the most loss budget?

2. Strategic Fit
   2.1 Which option reinforces Coupang's fulfillment, frequency, data, and membership flywheel?
   2.2 Which option reuses existing capabilities?
   2.3 Which option is a distant adjacency?

3. Execution Feasibility
   3.1 Which option has known operating capabilities?
   3.2 Which option requires new network, integration, or regulatory capabilities?
   3.3 Which option could distract management from the core?

4. Evidence Strength And Gates
   4.1 Which option has official initiative-specific evidence?
   4.2 Which option relies mostly on management commentary or inference?
   4.3 What evidence should trigger scale, hold, or constrain decisions?
```

## Success Criteria

A strong answer should:

- Rank the options under one consistent standard.
- Separate Product Commerce recovery from Developing Offerings investment drag.
- Avoid treating Developing Offerings as one generic growth bucket.
- Identify decision gates for uncertain options.
- Acknowledge where public data is insufficient.
