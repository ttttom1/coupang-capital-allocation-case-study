# Option Scorecard

## Method

The scorecard is a case-specific decision tool. It is not a universal finance formula and not a substitute for a full internal valuation model.

Public sources do not isolate option-level cash flows for Taiwan, Eats/WOW, or Farfetch. A full NPV/IRR model would require standalone revenue, gross margin, subsidy, fulfillment cost, cohort retention, and capital intensity data. The scorecard converts available evidence into an auditable prioritization rule.

## Formula

Each option receives a 1-5 score for five criteria. Higher is better.

```text
Capital Allocation Score =
  35% * Economics
+ 20% * Strategic Fit
+ 15% * Execution Feasibility
+ 20% * Evidence Strength
+ 10% * Reversibility
```

100-point version:

```text
Score / 100 =
  35 * Economics / 5
+ 20 * Strategic Fit / 5
+ 15 * Execution Feasibility / 5
+ 20 * Evidence Strength / 5
+ 10 * Reversibility / 5
```

## Weight Rationale

| Criterion | Weight | Rationale |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Economics | 35% | The core problem is margin drag and capital allocation. |
| Strategic Fit | 20% | Coupang should favor options that reinforce fulfillment, frequency, data, and membership. |
| Evidence Strength | 20% | Unsupported upside should not score like verified economics. |
| Execution Feasibility | 15% | Execution risk matters, but public data limits precision. |
| Reversibility | 10% | Staged options deserve credit when uncertainty is high. |

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Meaning |
| ---: | --- |
| 5 | Strong positive evidence; clear value creation; manageable risk. |
| 4 | Positive evidence with some open questions. |
| 3 | Mixed evidence; plausible but unproven. |
| 2 | Weak or negative evidence; high risk or unclear economics. |
| 1 | Materially unfavorable or unsupported. |

## Score Justification

### Product Commerce recovery: 89

Product Commerce is the profit engine. FY2025 Product Commerce adjusted EBITDA was $2.485B, and Q1 2026 still produced $358M despite temporary incident pressure. It also has direct strategic fit with fulfillment density, customer frequency, merchant network, and membership value.

It does not receive a perfect total score because Q1 recovery still needs proof, and Product Commerce is less reversible than optional growth bets.

### Taiwan expansion: 66

Taiwan has the strongest non-core growth signal and uses Coupang's core operating capabilities. Management commentary points to strong early response and retention.

The score is capped because standalone Taiwan revenue, gross profit, EBITDA, and cohort economics are not disclosed. Taiwan should scale in stages with unit-economics gates.

### Eats/WOW flywheel: 58.5

Eats/WOW has strong strategic fit because it can increase frequency, retention, and cross-category engagement.

The economics are unproven from public data. Contribution margin, subsidy intensity, delivery cost, incremental LTV, WOW member churn, and regulatory impact are not disclosed. This makes Eats/WOW a proof case, not a scale case.

### Farfetch normalization: 42

Farfetch is material, but the evidence base supports risk and materiality more than turnaround upside. The luxury marketplace adjacency is more distant from Coupang's core fulfillment flywheel, and public filings disclose broad acquisition and integration risks.

Farfetch should receive the strictest gate.

## Sensitivity Check

The recommendation is directionally stable. If Strategic Fit receives more weight, Taiwan and Eats/WOW improve, but Product Commerce remains first and Farfetch remains last. If Evidence Strength receives more weight, Taiwan and Eats/WOW are penalized for missing standalone economics, while Product Commerce remains the clear priority.
